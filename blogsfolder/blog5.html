Wireframes and UI Elements for a Professional and Personal Portfolio

This website was created as a portfolio to showcase my work and achievements so far. My design focuses on creating an intuitive and simple way to explore everything I’ve done. I aim to strike a balance between being professional and relaxed, as the site should represent me personally while still appealing to the people I want to showcase it to. Comparing those early wireframes with my current designs, I can see a significant improvement in how I approach both structure and user experience. My new wireframes are much more organized, featuring clearly defined sections and consistent layouts across pages. Each page now has a specific purpose and is properly titled, helping users know where they are and what to expect.

Goal Alignment
My goal has always been to create a portfolio that is both professional and reflective of my personal style, and I believe my current designs are much closer to achieving that balance.

Information Structure – Content Mapping
The pages I now include are:

  • Homepage: Introduction to who I am, with clear text about me and navigation to other sections.
  • Blogs Page: A space to share blog posts, with placeholders for text and blog titles.
  • Design Page: Dedicated to showcasing my design work, including text descriptions and images.
  • Projects Page: A gallery-style layout to highlight key projects with descriptions and visuals.
  • Profile Page: Focused on my background, achievements, and personal statement, with supporting images.

User Flow – Screens, Behaviour, and Decisions
Each page now connects logically to the others. I’ve added a navigation bar that is consistent across all pages — something that was missing from my early designs. This makes it much easier for users to move between pages without frustration. In redesigning the wireframes, I focused heavily on improving the user experience (UX). I considered how users would move through the site, ensuring that each page is accessible and that information is presented in a way that doesn’t overwhelm.

List and Explanation of Selected Interface Elements
  • A navigation bar that is always visible and consistent across all pages.
  • Icons for the coding languages I am proficient in, serving as visual cues for my technical skills.
  • Rounded corners used throughout to create a friendly and relaxed feel.
  • Standard components like a header and footer for a well-organized layout.
  • Clear contact options, making it easy for users to get in touch with me.
  • A sticky navigation bar that allows quick access to sections without scrolling back up.

Style Process Documentation
I haven’t finalized the typography yet, but I’m aiming for a clean, modern font that complements the soft pastel theme and ensures good readability. I have chosen a colour palette that will define the site's visual style:
  • Primary Colours: Deep Teal (#0F5257), Pastel Blue (#8DA9C4), Warm Red (#AA4465)
  • Accent and Background Colours:Soft Cream (#FAF0CA), Light Cream (#FAF8D4)
These colours are intended to create a calm and approachable vibe — ideal for a portfolio that is both professional and welcoming.

Reference Material
One of my main inspirations is Adham Dannaway's website.

What I like:
  • The clean and simple layout.
  • Effective use of space and minimalism, which keeps things uncluttered.
  • The homepage image that shifts between programming- and creative-focused visuals — a great way to represent a dual skill set.
What I don’t like:
  • Some sections feel too formal and robotic, which isn’t the vibe I’m going for. I want something more inviting and casual.
