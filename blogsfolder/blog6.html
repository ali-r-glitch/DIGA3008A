Designing a Portfolio: Color Palettes, Fonts, and UI Inspirations

I want a clear and functional website that displays both my engineering and digital arts achievements. For the visual elements, I will blend soft shapes and bold, geometric fonts to create a stark contrast. I plan to use rounded buttons and icons, paired with sharp, structured fonts. I’m still undecided on the exact fonts, but currently I'm considering Tektur and Arimo for body text (with a font weight of 0) and Barlow Condensed for headings. My colour palette will feature grey or black backgrounds, with bright accent colours and white or brightly coloured text. The images below show my main inspiration for the colour palette. I’m drawn to this style because I want to use bright colours against a dark background to enhance readability and create vivid, eye-catching visuals. This approach should result in a bold website that uses minimal colours and simple elements. The simplicity of this design will make it easier to maintain a cohesive and polished look. I find the use of sharp edges and bold imagery worls well for "Frag punk" as it creates a bold and intensive look. Howvere for the more soft and approachable side of my portfolio, I want to use softer shapes and colours.
